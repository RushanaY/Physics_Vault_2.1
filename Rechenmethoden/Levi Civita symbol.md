$$\epsilon_{i_1, i_2...j_n}^{j_1, j_2,...j_n} \equiv sgn(P_{i_1, i_2...j_n}^{j_1, j_2,...j_n} )$$
This is the fill notation, where the upper or one of the sequencies is assumed to be $1,2....$ and then the other one is the end sequence that needs an odd or even amount permutations to reach it. 
Therefore it is shorter to write $$\epsilon_{i_1, i_2, ..., i_n} \equiv \epsilon^{i_1, i_2, ..., i_n} \equiv sgn(P_{i_1, i_2...j_n}^{j_1, j_2,...j_n})$$and otherwise it is 0 (in case $i_n = j_m$) 
If the numbers go clockwise, then it's 1, if anti clockwise then -1.

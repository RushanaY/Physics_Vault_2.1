As told in the main article, the matrix is just a representation of the action of a linear map. And as any representation it can have as many faces as we want and need. In order to change between the matrices we use the [[transformation matrix]] $$A' = TAT^{-1}$$
It is very important to remember that we work here with matrix multiplication, which means that the calculation of this series of matrices starts from the end!
The same equation can be easily derived from the visual representation of the whole structure in form of the [[The square]]. 

This change of representation is also called the **similarity transformation**, because we change merely the "looks" of the matrix, but both $A'$ and $A$ will do the same action of the linear map.

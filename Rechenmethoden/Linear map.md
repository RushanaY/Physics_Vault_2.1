A linear map is connecting two vector spaces. In the simplest case, even without vectors, it is a  "function" that takes in an argument and applies some sort of computation onto it. In the case of this map, the argument is the vector and the "function" is a [[Matrix]].
A defining property and also a way to check linearity is $$F  (a\vec v + b \vec w) = a F(\vec v) + b F(\vec w)$$

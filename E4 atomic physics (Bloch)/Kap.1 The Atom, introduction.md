## 1.1.3. energy 
(if you are looking for numbers, those are in [[constants]])
Electrons are in the electric field of the nucleus and those electrons experience the **potential energy** $$|E_{pot}| \approx \int_{\infty}^{r_H} F ds = \frac{1}{4 \pi \epsilon_0} \frac{Ze^2}{r_H}$$
	-> for $Z=1$ and $r_H \approx 0.5 \cdot 10^{-10}m$ we get the energy $$|E_{pot}| \approx 10^{-18}J \approx 6eV$$
For the **kinetic energy** we assume that there is also $6eV$, which  means we assume the energy  is in the same size => **total energy** is then $E_{tot} = 12eV$ 

This is the **binding energy** of an atom


## 1.1.4.  thermal exitation 
it acts against the [[binding energy]] inside atom -> $k_B T = E_{tot}$ 
Knowing that $E_{tot} = 12eV$, we can calculate the temperature that is needed to split the atom, being stronger that the binding energy $$T \approx 140 \space 000 K$$
=> at this temeprature the electrons evaporate

## 1.1.7. Energy scale in an atom
### electrostatic interaction nucleus <=> hull
strongest interaction power -> comes from the electron moving in the field of the nucleus
between the positive $Ze$ of the nucleus and an electron from the hull
then the electron has the ground energy $$E_1 = - \frac{1}{2} Z^2 \alpha^2 mc^2$$
with $\alpha$ being the [[Fine structure constant]] 


Considering the **relativistic** effect -> electron is actually that fast 
- need [[heispenbergs uncertainty principle]] 
- rel. velocities possible for **heavy atoms** 
- for light one $Z \alpha << 1$ it is not the case 


### [[Spin orbit coupling]] (in many body stystem)
= relativistic effect 
Energy of a magneric dipol we have $$E = - \vec  \mu \cdot \vec B$$
with $\vec \mu$ being the [[magnetic moment]]
electron has spin and therfore a magnetic moment of the [[Bohrs Magneton]] 
leads to the splitting of the energy niveaus 


### [[hyper fine structure]] 
the [[nucleus]] also has [[magnetic moment]] $\mu_H$ 
electron orbits around the nucleus and induces a magnetic field. The magnetic moment of the nucleus itneracts with this magnetif field and it leads to more **splitting of energy nivaus** 
$$E_{hfc} = - \mu)H \cdot B_{el} = - \frac{1}{1836} \mu_B B_{el}$$

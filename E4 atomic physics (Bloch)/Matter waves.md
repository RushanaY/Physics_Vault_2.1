## Feynmann path integral 
splitting of the wave of an atom in two parts using an interferometer 
-> when two waves shift in relation to eachother, we get a **phase shift** 
this is calcualted by comparing the paths the waves take $$\delta = \frac{1}{\hbar} (S_1 - S_2) = \int_{C_1} k(r) dr - \int_{C_2} k(r) dr$$
from this we see, that the phaseshift can only come from -> different path or different potentials

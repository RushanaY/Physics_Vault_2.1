# interstellar medium (ISM)
- all the stuff between the stars 
- 15% of visible mass of a galaxy is in the interstellar medium 
## components 
- 99% gas with density $1 cm^{-3}$
- 1% dust with density $1 km^{-3}$ 

# phases 
## molecular clouds 
- appear from cooling down of hydrogen 
- is pretty rare 
- here stars get created 
## HI region 
- promordial hydrogen = was there since start of the universe => got directly made from electrons and protons from the primordial soup 
- dominates the ISM
- mainly visible across the galactical plane (is bascially the middle line) $\sim 200pc$

### neutral hydrogen 
- detectable though** **21 cm line** => reason are the Auswahlregeln of the hyperfine structure 
	- spontanious transition rate $$A = 2.9 \times 10^{-15} \frac{1}{s}$$
	- life time $$\tau = A^{-1} = 1.1 \times 10^7 yr$$

## HII region 
- **ionised gas** that appears around hot, big, joung stars => we call this [[Stromgren spheres]]
	- important: stars should have very strong UV radiation ($\lambda < 91nm$), spectral type O or eraly B 
- even visible like dots on the sky 
- ionisation 
	- reaction of a photon with neutral hydrogen  $$H^0 + \gamma \to p + e^-$$
	- mittlere freie Weglänge $$l_{13,6eV} \approx \frac{0.05pc}{\frac{n_{H^0}}{1cm^{-3}}}$$
	- afterwards the recombination $$e^- + p \to H^0 + \gamma + \gamma + ... $$
	- usually in **Balmer series** 
- both dark and light clouds are usually very close to eachother => two phases of star creation close to eachother 
	- dark cloud -> dust protects gas cloud  from destructive UV light => no ionisation in the cloud 
	-

- makes up a very small portion of the ISM 
- on the surface of dust particle  -> neutral hydrogen HI can transition into H_2
- => main task of dust to get atoms together 
- after particle gets warmed up => H_2 freigesetzt

# physics describtion 
density $$n = \frac{\rho}{\overline \mu m_H}$$
pressure $$P = n \cdot k_B \cdot T$$
sound velocity $$c_s = \sqrt{\frac{dP}{d \rho}} \approx 1 km \space s^{-1} \cdot \sqrt{\frac{1}{\overline \mu} \cdot \frac{T}{100K}}$$
-> velocity with which matter moves 


# stability 
- the ISM exists in a "flux", going from hot and thin (gas) to cold and thick (dust)
- condition for stability: **pressure equillibrium** 
- using ideal gas law, I guess: $$P = n \cdot k_B \cdot T \Rightarrow n \cdot T = const \approx 1-^3 - 10^4 K \space cm^{-3}$$
- BUT: it is not perfectly stationary - example of creation of a O star (very bright, usually in places of active star formation, blue light)
	- O star appears => ionisation of ISM around it => temperature increases drasitcally => particle density goes down? => density equillibrium distorted => **expansion of HII region**

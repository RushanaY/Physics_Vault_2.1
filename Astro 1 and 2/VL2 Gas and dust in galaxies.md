# components of interstellar medium

- 15% of visible matter is in ISM (interstellar medium), which is made up from
	- $\sim 99\%$ different kinds of gas with mean density $\sim 1cm^{-3}$
	- $\sim 1 \%$ dust with mean density $1 km^{-3}$
- is made up from 5 phases:
	- molecular coulds
		- from cooling down of atomic hydrigen
		- place where stars start, I think 
	- [[HI region]]s 
		- promoridal, hydrigen from the beginning of the universe
	- [[HII region]]s
		- bubbles of [[ionised gas]] that form around energy rich stars ([[Strömgren sphären]])
	- [[hot gas]] 
		- ionised gas from supernove exposions (is hotter than)
	- dust 
		- grime from the stars (I guess after they burn out)

The connection of the phases:
atomic **[[HI gas]]** comes from the neighbourhood of the galxy. Cooling down and contracting it becomes **hydrigen gas**. From this gas young stars form and the gas gets ionised, becoming **ionised [[HII gas]]**. The stars burn out at some point, protons and electrons recombine and break down back into **HI gas**.

Physical describtion:
1. **Density** $\Rightarrow$ number of particles $$n = \frac{\rho}{\overline{\mu} m_H}$$
2. **Pressure** ([[ideal gas]] assumed)$$P = n \cdot k_B \cdot T$$
3. **speed of sound** 
$$c_S \equiv \sqrt{\frac{dP}{d\rho}} \approx 
\sqrt{\frac{n k_B T}{\overline{\mu} \, m_H \, n}} 
\approx 1~\text{km s}^{-1} \sqrt{\frac{1}{\overline{\mu}} \frac{T}{100\,\text{K}}}
$$

Stability of the ISM
Probably guaranteed through the cycle of phases from cold and dense to hot and thin gas. 
Guaranteed with the criterium: $$ P= nk_B T \Rightarrow n \cdot T = const$$then we can define the constantL $n \cdot T \approx 10^3 - 10^4 K cm^{-3}$

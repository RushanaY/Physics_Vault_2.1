(quasi stellar radio source) also QSO 
		- very powerful x rays point source (very very bright, better visible than a galaxy) -> observed visually 
		- a lot of variability in emitted frequencies
		- a lot of reddening (because they can be observed from much further away)
		- emitted rays are polarized (up to 30% in elongated x ray sources)
		- the spectrum can partly be described with ($\alpha =0$ - flat spectrum of very compact componetns liek nulceus, $\alpha \sim 0.7$ - for elongated compoennts)  $$S_{\nu} \propto \nu^{-\alpha}$$ 
		- characteristic frequenxy of the mission: $$v = \frac{3 \gamma^2 e B}{4 \pi m_2 c} \approx 4.2 \times 10^6 \gamma^2 (\frac{B}{1G}) Hz$$where $\gamma$ is the Lorentz factor

start with the physics behind the string telephone 
before I start showing how I implemented those aspects in my project I want to quickly go over the ways I determine the succes of my project 
For me the most important were the loudness and the clearness of the sound or more importantly speech
SNR, RMS  and the difference to the clean slate show how much Louder the noise in the can is, than just listening to the sound without a proper string telephone 
the clarity is in the clarity of speech, which shows how distinctive the hgher frequency consonants are on the background of the lower frequency vowels 
lastly the more overtones a sound has, the more "colour" it has

Now I start with the membrane 
those are the modes that the circular membrane gets from external sound 
My telephone has a drum head membrane, that is 0.254mm thick mylar 
measuring the overtones of a 255Hz sound we can see how the membrane transmits those much better, having them being louder, than the metal one
on top of that the membrane is reinforced with a metall gormmets to reduce the risk of ripping of the membrane and act against the scratching of the string against the sharp edge of the membrane cut 

Next comes the string that gets the vibration from the membrane 
the membrane induces longitudinal and tranversal waves inside the string. In my project it is mainly the tranversal wave that is responsible for the enrgy transmission, because the used dyneema string has a very small elasitcity and can't compress and expand for the longitudinal wave
During the experient I have tested 4 types of thickness strings of Dyneema and the results comparing SNR, clearness of speech and loundess though the comparison with the clean slate, in the diagramm. It is visible that the thinnest and lightest string has the best results overall

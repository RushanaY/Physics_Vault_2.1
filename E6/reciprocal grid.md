# reciprocal grid
$$\vec G = h \vec b_1 + k \vec b_2 + l \vec b_3$$
with the condition: $$\vec b_i \vec a_j = 2 \pi \delta_{ij}$$
mathematecally $$\vec b_1 = 2 \pi \frac{(\vec a_2 \times \vec a_3)}{\vec a_1 \cdot (\vec a_2 \times \vec a_3)}$$$$\vec b_2 = 2 \pi \frac{(\vec a_3 \times \vec a_1)}{\vec a_1 \cdot (\vec a_2 \times \vec a_3)}$$
$$\vec b_3 = 2 \pi \frac{(\vec a_1 \times \vec a_2)}{\vec a_1 \cdot (\vec a_2 \times \vec a_3)}$$

# application
- we can build a new basis out of $\vec b_i$ 
- If normal grid was bcc, then the reciprocal will be fcc and the other way round
- if it's simple cubic, then evrything stays the same

at **high** temperatures $T>> \Theta_D$  ([[debye temperature]])we get :$$\sigma_{ph}^{-1} \sim \rho_{ph} \sim T$$
at **low** temperatures $T<< \Theta_D$ we get :$$\rho_{ph} \sim T^5$$
The difference comes from the fact that at smal lmomentum changes (= small scattering angles) the angle is very important and therefore we need to consider the Gewichtsfaktor $(1 - \cos (\xi)) \sim T^2$$

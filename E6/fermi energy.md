$$E_F = \frac{\hbar k_F^2}{2m} \simeq 4 eV$$




Important: 
- total energy of a system is $$E_{ges} = 2 \sum_{k\leq k_F} \frac{\hbar^2 k^2}{2m}$$
	- and for big volumes we can use the integral instad of the sum sign $$E_{ges} = 2 \frac{V}{(2 \pi)^3} \int_0^{k_F} \frac{\hbar^2k^2}{2m} d^3l = 2 \frac{V}{(2 \pi)^3} \int_0^{k_F} \frac{\hbar^2 k^2}{2m} 4 \pi k^2 dk = \frac{V}{10 \pi^2}\frac{\hbar^2}{m} k_F^5$$
		using the formular for partcile density and [[fermi wave number]] we get the energy of a particle being $$\frac{E_{ges}}{N} = \frac{3}{5} E_F = \frac{3}{5} k_B T_F$$

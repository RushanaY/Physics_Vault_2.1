- for $T>0$ we get the [[Fermi dirac distribution]] being $$f_{FD} (E) = \frac{1}{e^{\frac{E- \mu}{k_B T}}+1}$$
	with $\mu$ being the [[chemical potential]]	

Graph describtion: 
- $T=0K$ : all states under $E = \mu = E_F$ are filled and all states above that are empty 
- $T>0K$ : electrons can be thermically exited => they leave states under $E_F$ and fill the states above it 
- at room temperature we have for metals $\frac{k_B T}{E_F} \approx 10^{-2}$ => barely $1\%$ of electrons participate in this repositioning 
Generally: the hotter it gets, the more electrons get "repositioned" 
-> this repositioning happens in the area of $k_B T$ around $E_F$

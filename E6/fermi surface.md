In [[electric conductivity]]:
- the fermi surface explains why the electric conductivuty equation usess the [[fermi velocity]] and not the drift velocity (-> in [[Drude modell]] we have electrons with velociteis of  $\nu_D << \nu_F$, while the [[Sommerfeld model]] has a small number of electrons that move with $\nu_F$ => Drude is technically wrong, because it is deply classical)
	- only electrons on the fermi surface can participate in scattering  -> because under the surface there are no free states 
	- those electrons, that participate in scattering have the velocity $$|\vec \nu| \approx \nu_F$$
	- typical shift in the [[fermi sphere]] is $\frac{\partial k}{k_F} \simeq 10^{-5}$

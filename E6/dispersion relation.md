[[Quasiimpuls (crystal momentum)]] and $\omega$ are related, which can be seen in the [[harmonic approximation]], so the fromular for $\omega$ is the dispersion relation 

If we have a normal [[grid constant]] $a$ and therefore the [[reciprocal grid]] constant $\frac{2 \pi}{a}$ 

=> the first [[brillouin zone]] has the length of the resirocal grid and goes from $- \frac{\pi}{a} \leq q \leq \frac{\pi}{a}$ (for $\omega =0$ and $q=0$) 
-  all information about the relation are contained inside the first  B. zone 

-> $\omega_{max}$ at the edges of the zone for $q = \pm \frac{\pi}{a}$ 

-> $\omega$ becomes linear for small $q$ => $\sin (\frac{qa}{2}) \approx \frac{qa}{2}$ 
	from this linear correlation $\omega = const \cdot q$ we can get the speed of sound

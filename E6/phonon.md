It is possible because of the [[wave particle duality]]. The wave here has particle properties, so therefore is also equipped with :
- momentum $$\vec p = \hbar \vec q$$
- energy $$E = \hbar \omega$$
The oscillation gets exited into the state $n_{\vec q r}$ = creation of a $n_{\vec q r}$ phonon of type $r$ and wave vector $\vec q$ 
==the higher the oscillation, the more phonons of this type exist== 


To get the probability of a phonon being in a position, whe use the [[Bose Einstein distribution]] 

### transversal modes 
-> in 3D the $u (\vec r)$ describes the displacement of whole atomic planes => those planes can be perpendicular to the [[wave vector]] = [[transversal modes]] 

- in total: 3 Modes = 1 [[longtitude|longitudinal]] and 2 transversal
- for all modes for very small $q$ we have $\omega = \nu_{schall} \cdot q$ -> acoustic modes 
	- 2 trasncersal acoustic (TA) modes 
	- 1 longitudinal acoustic (LA) modes 

Generally for N atoms we have: 
- 3N phonon branches -> 3 being **acoustic** and the rest are **optical**  ones

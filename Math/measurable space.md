If we have two measurable spaces $(X, \mathcal{A})$ and $(Y, \mathcal{B})$, then a [[map]]  $f: X \to Y$ is [[A-B mesurable]] if: 
- $f^{-1}(B) \in \mathcal{A}$ for all $B \in \mathcal{B}$ (*this somehwhat looks like a bijection*)

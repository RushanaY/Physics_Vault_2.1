When analyzing nuclear and particle reactions, we are essentially playing the role of a "detective." We have a set of strict laws—**Conservation Laws**—that the universe must obey. If a reaction breaks even one of these laws, it is **Forbidden**. If it obeys all of them, it is generally **Allowed**.

Here are the "Big Three" laws we will check for every single line:

1. **Conservation of Charge ($Q$):** The total electric charge must be the same on both sides.
    
2. **Conservation of Baryon Number ($B$):** Baryons (like protons and neutrons) have $B=+1$. Antibaryons have $B=-1$. Non-baryons (like electrons or pions) have $B=0$. The total $B$ must match.
    
3. **Conservation of Lepton Number ($L$):** Leptons (electrons, neutrinos) have $L=+1$. Anti-leptons have $L=-1$. We must conserve the total count.
    

Let’s analyze them one by one!

### (a) $^{87}_{37}\text{Rb} \rightarrow ^{87}_{38}\text{Sr} + e^-$

- **Analysis:**
    
    - **Charge ($Q$):** Left is $37$. Right is $38 + (-1) = 37$. (Check $\checkmark$)
        
    - **Baryon Number ($B$):** Left is $87$. Right is $87$. (Check $\checkmark$)
        
    - **Lepton Number ($L$):** Left is $0$. Right has an electron ($e^-$), which has $L_e = +1$. So, $0 \rightarrow 1$. **VIOLATION!**
        
- **Verdict:** **Forbidden (Nicht erlaubt)**
    
- **Reason:** **Violation of Lepton Number Conservation.**
    
    - _Professor's Note:_ This looks very close to a standard "Beta-minus decay." For this to be allowed, we would need to add an **electron antineutrino** ($\bar{\nu}_e$) to the right side to cancel out the electron's lepton number ($1 + (-1) = 0$).
        

### (b) $^{222}_{88}\text{Ra} \rightarrow ^{208}_{82}\text{Pb} + ^{14}_{6}\text{C}$

- **Analysis:**
    
    - **Charge ($Q$):** Left is $88$. Right is $82 + 6 = 88$. (Check $\checkmark$)
        
    - **Mass Number ($A$):** Left is $222$. Right is $208 + 14 = 222$. (Check $\checkmark$)
        
    - **Leptons:** None involved.
        
- **Verdict:** **Allowed (Erlaubt)**
    
- **Type:** **Cluster Decay (Nuclear Reaction)**
    
    - _Professor's Note:_ This is a fascinating and rare type of radioactivity called "Cluster Decay," where a nucleus emits a chunk larger than an alpha particle (in this case, a Carbon-14 nucleus). It is mediated by the **Strong Interaction** (tunneling effect).
        

### (c) $^{235}_{92}\text{U} \rightarrow ^{236}_{93}\text{Np} + \bar{p}$

- **Analysis:**
    
    - **Charge ($Q$):** Left is $92$. Right is Neptunium ($93$) + Antiproton ($-1$) = $92$. (Check $\checkmark$)
        
    - **Baryon Number ($B$):** Left is $235$. Right is $236$ (from Np) + $(-1)$ (from antiproton) = $235$. (Check $\checkmark$)
        
    - **Energy Considerations:** Even though the "counting" numbers balance, we have to look at energy. An antiproton ($\bar{p}$) has a huge mass (approx 938 MeV). You cannot spontaneously create that much mass _and_ a heavier nucleus (Np is heavier than U) out of a lighter U-235 nucleus. The parent nucleus does not have enough mass-energy to create these products.
        
- **Verdict:** **Forbidden (Nicht erlaubt)**
    
- **Reason:** **Violation of Conservation of Energy.**
    
    - _Professor's Note:_ While the quantum numbers technically balance, you cannot get "something from nothing." The mass of the products is significantly higher than the mass of the reactant. Spontaneous decay requires the products to be lighter (releasing energy).
        

### (d) $e^+ + e^- \rightarrow n + n$

- **Analysis:**
    
    - **Charge ($Q$):** Left is $(+1) + (-1) = 0$. Right is $0 + 0 = 0$. (Check $\checkmark$)
        
    - **Lepton Number ($L$):** Left is $(-1) + (+1) = 0$. Right is $0$. (Check $\checkmark$)
        
    - **Baryon Number ($B$):** Left is $0$ (leptons are not baryons). Right is a neutron ($+1$) + neutron ($+1$) = **$+2$**. **VIOLATION!**
        
- **Verdict:** **Forbidden (Nicht erlaubt)**
    
- **Reason:** **Violation of Baryon Number Conservation.**
    
    - _Professor's Note:_ You cannot turn pure energy (or leptons) into matter (baryons) without also creating antimatter to balance it out. If the reaction were $e^+ e^- \rightarrow n + \bar{n}$ (neutron + antineutron), it would conserve Baryon number (though you'd need a lot of energy!).
        

### (e) $^{40}_{18}\text{Ar}^* \rightarrow ^{40}_{18}\text{Ar} + \gamma$

- **Analysis:**
    
    - The asterisk ($^*$) on the Argon indicates it is in an excited energy state.
        
    - It releases a photon ($\gamma$) and drops to the ground state.
        
    - Protons, neutrons, and electrons are unchanged.
        
- **Verdict:** **Allowed (Erlaubt)**
    
- **Type:** **Electromagnetic Interaction (Gamma Decay)**
    
    - _Professor's Note:_ This is the nuclear equivalent of an atom emitting light when an electron jumps down an energy level. Here, the protons/neutrons re-arrange, emitting a high-energy gamma ray.
        

### (f) $p + e^- \rightarrow n + \nu_e$

- **Analysis:**
    
    - **Charge ($Q$):** Left is $+1 + (-1) = 0$. Right is $0 + 0 = 0$. (Check $\checkmark$)
        
    - **Baryon Number ($B$):** Left is $1$ (proton). Right is $1$ (neutron). (Check $\checkmark$)
        
    - **Lepton Number ($L_e$):** Left is $+1$ (electron). Right is $+1$ (electron neutrino). (Check $\checkmark$)
        
- **Verdict:** **Allowed (Erlaubt)**
    
- **Type:** **Weak Interaction**
    
    - _Professor's Note:_ This reaction is known as **Electron Capture** (or inverse beta decay). Whenever you see neutrinos ($\nu$) involved with changing particle "flavors" (proton turning to neutron), it is almost always the **Weak Interaction**.
        

### (g) $\pi^0 \rightarrow \gamma + \gamma$

- **Analysis:**
    
    - **Charge ($Q$):** $0 \rightarrow 0$. (Check $\checkmark$)
        
    - **Baryon/Lepton:** All are 0. (Check $\checkmark$)
        
    - **Quark Content:** The hint tells us $\pi^0$ is a mix of $|u\bar{u}\rangle$ and $|d\bar{d}\rangle$.
        
    - This is a particle acting as its own antiparticle and annihilating into pure energy (photons).
        
- **Verdict:** **Allowed (Erlaubt)**
    
- **Type:** **Electromagnetic Interaction**
    
    - _Professor's Note:_ Decays into photons are the signature of the Electromagnetic force. The neutral pion has a very short lifetime because its internal quarks can annihilate each other via electromagnetism.
        

### (h) $\Delta^{++} \rightarrow p + \pi^+$

- **Analysis:**
    
    - **Charge ($Q$):** Left is $+2$ ($\Delta^{++}$). Right is $+1$ ($p$) $+ 1$ ($\pi^+$) $= +2$. (Check $\checkmark$)
        
    - **Baryon Number ($B$):** Left is $+1$ ($\Delta^{++}$ is a baryon). Right is $+1$ ($p$) $+ 0$ ($\pi^+$) $= +1$. (Check $\checkmark$)
        
    - **Quark Flow:**
        
        - Left: $\Delta^{++}$ is $|uuu\rangle$.
            
        - Right: Proton is $|uud\rangle$, Pion is $|u\bar{d}\rangle$.
            
        - Reaction: $|uuu\rangle \rightarrow |uud\rangle + |u\bar{d}\rangle$.
            
        - If you look closely, we started with 3 Up quarks. We ended with 3 Up quarks plus a Down/Anti-Down pair ($d\bar{d}$).
            
    - Since quarks are conserved and we just created a particle-antiparticle pair from the "gluon field" (energy), this is a standard strong decay.
        
- **Verdict:** **Allowed (Erlaubt)**
    
- **Type:** **Strong Interaction**
    
    - _Professor's Note:_ The $\Delta$ particle is a "resonance"—an extremely short-lived excited state of the proton. Because the decay products ($p, \pi$) are hadrons and no quark flavors had to change (we just popped a pair out of the vacuum), this happens via the **Strong Interaction**.

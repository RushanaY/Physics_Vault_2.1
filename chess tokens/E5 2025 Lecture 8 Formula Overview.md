

Class is reaching the final frontier! We have moved past elastic scattering (where the proton stays whole) into **Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS)**. In DIS, the electron hits the proton so hard that it literally shatters it, revealing the **quarks** inside.

In an exam, you’ll be asked to describe the internal structure of the proton and how these "scaling variables" prove that protons aren't solid blobs, but are made of point-like constituents.

---

## 1. Bjorken Scaling Variable ($x$)

$$x := \frac{Q^2}{2P \cdot q} = \frac{Q^2}{2M\nu}$$

This is perhaps the most important variable in high-energy physics.

- **$Q^2$:** The "hardness" or resolution of the hit (four-momentum transfer).
    
- **$M$:** The mass of the proton.
    
- **$\nu$ (Nu):** The energy lost by the electron in the lab frame ($E - E'$).
    
- **The Physical Meaning:** In the "Infinite Momentum Frame," **$x$ is the fraction of the proton's total momentum carried by the single quark that got hit**.
    
- **The Range:** $0 \leq x \leq 1$. If $x=1$, it was an elastic collision (the whole proton moved). If $x$ is small, the electron hit a tiny piece of the proton's "sea" of particles.
    

---

## 2. Inelasticity ($y$)

$$y := \frac{P \cdot q}{P \cdot k} = \frac{\nu}{E} = \frac{E - E'}{E}$$

This tells us how much of the electron's initial energy was dumped into the proton.

- **$E$:** Initial energy of the electron.
    
- **$E'$:** Final energy of the electron.
    
- **Range:** $0 \leq y \leq 1$.
    
- **Exam Strategy:** If $y$ is close to 1, the electron lost almost all its energy to the interaction. This usually happens in very "deep" collisions.
    

---

## 3. Structure Functions ($F_1$ and $F_2$)

$$\frac{d^2\sigma}{dx dQ^2} = \pi \left( \frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega} \right)_{Rutherford} \cdot \frac{c^2}{E^2} \left[ \left(1-y-\frac{Mc^2 xy}{2E}\right) \frac{F_2(x, Q^2)}{x} + y^2 F_1(x, Q^2) \right]$$

When the proton shatters, we can no longer use simple Form Factors. We use **Structure Functions**.

- **$F_1(x, Q^2)$:** Relates to the magnetic interaction with the quarks.
    
- **$F_2(x, Q^2)$:** Relates to the electric charge distribution of the quarks.
    
- **Bjorken Scaling:** For a long time, we thought these functions depended on $Q^2$. However, experiments showed that at high energies, they only depend on **$x$**. This proved that quarks are **point-like** particles!.
    

---

## 4. The Callan-Gross Relation

$$2x F_1(x, Q^2) = F_2(x, Q^2)$$

This is a "Golden Formula" for exams because it reveals the **Spin** of the quarks.

- **The Physics:** This equality only holds true if the particles inside the proton have **Spin $1/2$**.
    
- **Exam Question:** "What does the Callan-Gross relation tell us about the constituents of the proton?"
    
- **How to answer:** It proves that the "partons" (quarks) inside the proton are fermions with spin $1/2$. If they were spin 0, $F_1$ would be zero.
    

---

### Summary of DIS for your Exam:

- **$x$:** The momentum fraction of the struck quark.
    
- **$y$:** The fraction of energy lost by the electron.
    
- **Scaling:** Proves quarks are point-like.
    
- **Callan-Gross:** Proves quarks have spin $1/2$.

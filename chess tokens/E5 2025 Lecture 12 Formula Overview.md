In the 1960s, physicists realized that Electromagnetism and the Weak Force aren't separate entities, but two different "shades" of a single, unified force. These final formulas describe the mathematical machinery that ties them together.

## 1. The Unified Fermi Constant

$$G_F = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{8} \frac{g^2}{M_W^2 c^4} (\hbar c)^3$$

Earlier, we saw $G_F$ as a simple number. Now, we see its "ingredients."

- **$g$:** The fundamental weak coupling constant (the "strength" of the weak charge).
    
- **$M_W$:** The mass of the $W$ boson.
    
- **The Physics:** This formula shows why the Weak force feels weak at low energies. It’s not because the coupling ($g$) is small—it’s because the $W$ boson is so incredibly **heavy** ($M_W$). Imagine trying to throw a medicine ball ($W$ boson) compared to a ping-pong ball (photon); the "heaviness" makes the exchange much harder and thus "weaker" at long distances.
    

---

## 2. The Weinberg Angle (Electroweak Mixing)

$$|\gamma\rangle = \sin \theta_w |W^0\rangle + \cos \theta_w |B^0\rangle$$

$$|Z^0\rangle = \cos \theta_w |W^0\rangle - \sin \theta_w |B^0\rangle$$

This is the "Magic Trick" of the Standard Model. Nature starts with two neutral fields ($W^0$ and $B^0$), but they "mix" together to create the particles we actually see in the lab.

- **$\theta_w$:** The **Weinberg Angle** (or Weak Mixing Angle).
    
- **$|\gamma\rangle$:** The **Photon**. It is a specific mixture that remains massless.
    
- **$|Z^0\rangle$:** The **$Z$ boson**. It is the "heavy cousin" that gains mass through the Higgs mechanism.
    

**Exam Question:** "What is the physical significance of the Weinberg angle?"

**How to answer:** It represents the degree of mixing between the electromagnetic and weak interactions. It determines the relative masses of the $W$ and $Z$ bosons and how they couple to matter.

---

## 3. Relation of Charges

$$g \sin \theta_w = g' \cos \theta_w = e$$

This confirms the unification. It links the weak charges ($g$ and $g'$) directly to the electric charge ($e$).

- **$e$:** The elementary charge ($1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ C).
    
- **The Insight:** This formula proves that the "strength" of electricity is just a projection of the unified electroweak force.
    

---

## 4. The Z-Boson Resonance

$$M_Z = 91.1876 \pm 0.0021 \text{ GeV}$$

$$\Gamma_Z = \frac{\hbar}{\tau_z} = 2.4952 \pm 0.0023 \text{ GeV}$$

These are the experimental "fingerprints" of the $Z$ boson, often measured in $e^+ e^-$ colliders.

- **$M_Z$:** The mass of the $Z$ boson. It is nearly 100 times heavier than a proton!.
    
- **$\Gamma_Z$ (Breite der Resonanz):** The **Decay Width**.
    
- **The Physics:** According to the **Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle**, a particle that lives for a very short time ($\tau_z$) has an "uncertain" mass. The "Width" ($\Gamma_Z$) tells us how fast the particle decays. By measuring this width very precisely, physicists were able to prove there are exactly **three generations of light neutrinos** in the universe.
